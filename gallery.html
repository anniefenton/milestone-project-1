Commonly Foraged Plants

Broadleaf Plantain

Found in parks and fields. The leaves decrease inflammation and the seeds promote digestive health. Use the leaves next time you get a nettle sting!

Blackberry

Delicious and easy to forage! The berries ripen in late summer and can be eaten raw, or used in cooking and making conserves.

Meadowsweet

Found in fields and marshes. The honey, vanilla, and almond scent of this plant can be used to flavour foods in cooking.

Wild Garlic

This plant blooms in Spring - you'll smell it before you see it! Use in fresh salads, or to flavour your cooking.

Sloe Berry

These can be found in heathland in Autumn time. Pair the berries with plenty of sugar to infuse gin.

Magnolia

Stay away from the berries, but you can eat the petals! Use to flavour your cooking for a sweet ginger taste.

Never munch on a hunch!
